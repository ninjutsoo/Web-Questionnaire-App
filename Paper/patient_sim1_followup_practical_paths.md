# Practical Follow-Up Paths After patient-sim1

Validated research snapshot: June 16, 2026. Scope: patient simulation for stress testing, risk assessment, robustness, bias/fairness, safety, and evaluation of healthcare AI. This report intentionally does not recommend NHATS as a primary dataset because the official NHATS site currently states that data files are temporarily unavailable.

## 1. Executive Summary

The best practical follow-up is not another general virtual patient. The publishable gap is a **controlled patient-profile stress-testing framework** that uses clear-access datasets, varies patient expression and behavior counterfactually, and measures how a healthcare AI system fails across retrieval, safety escalation, recommendation consistency, calibration, and subgroup fairness.

Most nearby work now covers one of three pieces: patient/dialogue simulation, agentic clinical environments, or static safety/fairness benchmarks. patient-sim1 is strong because it combines EHR-grounded medical profiles, linguistic variation, behavioral variation, and downstream AI-system evaluation. Its main open gaps are ecological validation against real conversations, broader clinical tasks, stronger safety escalation tasks, and reproducible datasets outside All of Us.

Best next build:

1. Use **DDXPlus + Synthea** for a fast public MVP with explicit ground truth and controllable patient states.
2. Add **MIMIC-IV-ED/Note** if credentialed access is available, because it enables real ED profiles, notes, triage, medications, and outcomes.
3. Test an **interactive diagnostic or triage agent**, not only a chatbot answerer.
4. Stress with **counterfactual health-literacy, recall, emotional state, adversarial/minimizing behavior, comorbidity, and rare/edge-case profiles**.
5. Validate the simulator against **MTS-Dialog, MedDialog, ACI-Bench, and clinician review**, because patient-sim1 explicitly lacks real-conversation validation.

## 2. Deep Analysis of patient-sim1

**Seed paper:** `Paper/Patient-Simulation-Papers/patient-sim1.txt`, "Advancing AI Trustworthiness Through Patient Simulation: Risk Assessment of Conversational Agents for Antidepressant Selection."

**Goal:** Build a scalable patient simulator for risk assessment of conversational healthcare AI, especially whether performance remains equitable under medical complexity, health literacy variation, and difficult interaction styles.

**Dataset:** All of Us Research Program Registered Tier v8. The MDD cohort includes 58,446 participants, 466,752 antidepressant trials, 18,471 diagnoses, 2,642 medications, and 5,001 procedures. Antidepressant response is defined as at least 10 weeks on an antidepressant without switching or augmentation. Simulator evaluation focuses on fluoxetine, sertraline, trazodone, and duloxetine.

**Simulator:** Three profile layers:

- Medical profiles: All of Us EHR-derived histories generated by MAGI with top-K outcome predictors, risk-ratio gating, independence screening, and residual diversity expansion.
- Linguistic profiles: Limited, Functional, and Proficient health literacy plus Depression and Illness Anxiety communication styles.
- Behavioral profiles: Structured/Cooperative, Distracted/Unfocused, and Adversarial/Combative.

**AI system tested:** A conversational AI Decision Aid for antidepressant selection. It conducts structured intake, normalizes retrieved concepts, and uses MAGI to recommend antidepressants.

**Metrics:** Medical concept fidelity, human and LLM judge agreement, profile classification agreement, FKGL readability, response length, medical term density, depression score, topical similarity, toxicity, concept recall, Rank-1/Top-20 retrieval, and downstream recommendation F1.

**Key results:** Across 500 simulated conversations, Rank-1 retrieval degrades monotonically by health literacy: Limited 47.6%, Functional 69.6%, Proficient 81.9%. Downstream recommendation F1 also drops for low-literacy patients. Medical concept fidelity is high: 96.61% accurate across 8,210 expressed concepts, with human-human kappa 0.73 and human-LLM kappa 0.78. Behavioral profiles are highly distinguishable, while linguistic profiles are moderately distinguishable.

**Limitations:** No validation against real patient conversations; single clinical task; structured EHR only; only four antidepressants evaluated in the main simulator experiment; risk-ratio independence may exclude valid coupled comorbidities.

## 3. Related Work from patient-sim1

patient-sim1 organizes related work into four useful categories:

1. **User simulation:** General dialogue, information access, recommender, and LLM user simulators show how to automate interactive evaluation but are not healthcare-specific.
2. **Data-driven medical realism:** Synthea, SynSys, GAN/EHR methods, EHR-Safe, and SimSUM/SynSUM generate synthetic or EHR-grounded patient records, but often do not model multi-turn behavior or linguistic diversity.
3. **Behavioral and linguistic modeling:** PATIENT-psi, PatientSim, SFMSS, and challenging-patient simulators model persona, emotion, or behavior, but many are education-focused or weakly tied to downstream AI risk.
4. **Workflow/procedural control:** Multi-agent outpatient or hospital simulations model roles and workflow, but usually sacrifice fine-grained linguistic and behavioral stress variables.

patient-sim1 says the missing piece is a unified risk-assessment simulator with **medical realism + linguistic diversity + behavioral variation + traceable risk mapping**.

## 4. Additional Related Work Found

Recent 2024-2026 work expands the field in five directions:

- **Patient simulators:** PatientSim, AIPatient, PATIENT-psi, Roleplay-doh, Adaptive-VP, CureFun, and LLM virtual patient systems.
- **Interactive clinical-agent benchmarks:** AgentClinic, AI Hospital, MMD-Eval, MedAgentBench, SDBench, and AMIE-style diagnostic conversations.
- **Healthcare LLM evaluation:** HealthBench, HealthBench Professional, MedHELM, CRAFT-MD, CSEDB, and PatientSafetyBench/MedRiskEval.
- **Synthetic records and dialogue:** Synthea, EHR-Safe, SimSUM/SynSUM, NoteChat, SynDial, MedDialog, MTS-Dialog, and ACI-Bench.
- **Stress/fairness/safety:** Counterfactual demographic vignettes, red-team clinical prompts, adversarial patient simulations, mental-health safety scenarios, and bias evaluation frameworks.

Uncertain claim: several 2025-2026 preprints advertise public resources, but actual dataset/code availability varies. Treat arXiv-only stress-testing work as useful positioning, not a dependable foundation, until code/data are confirmed.

## 8. Best Practical Path to Take

Build **a reproducible, public-data patient simulation benchmark for diagnostic/triage AI stress testing**.

Recommended dataset stack:

1. **DDXPlus** for explicit symptom, antecedent, pathology, and differential-diagnosis labels.
2. **Synthea** for longitudinal EHR context and FHIR-like records.
3. **HealthBench rubrics** as a model for physician-style scoring, not as the primary patient-state source.
4. **MIMIC-IV-ED/Note** as the stronger extension if credentialed access is available.

Recommended AI system:

- An interactive diagnostic or triage agent that asks questions, normalizes patient statements, updates a differential, decides whether to escalate, and gives a final diagnosis or next-step recommendation.

Recommended contribution:

- A simulator that keeps the hidden clinical state constant while systematically varying expression, recall, behavior, and safety disclosure timing.
- A stress-test suite showing where the same AI changes its decision, confidence, or escalation behavior for equivalent patients.

## 8A. Novelty Stress Test and Reviewer Defense

**Novelty thesis:** The contribution is not another virtual patient simulator. It is a **paired counterfactual stress-testing protocol** where the unit of analysis is a case family: the same hidden clinical state is held fixed while patient-profile variables are systematically changed to test whether diagnosis, differential, escalation, questioning, confidence, and explanation remain clinically appropriate.

Do not frame the paper as "we build more realistic synthetic patients." Frame it as: **we build a controlled robustness assay for clinical AI agents using simulated patients as the intervention mechanism.**

### Why This Is Not Just Another Patient Simulator

| Comparator | What it already does | Why the proposed work is different | Evidence needed to make the distinction hold |
|---|---|---|---|
| patient-sim1 | EHR-grounded antidepressant patient simulator with medical, linguistic, and behavioral profiles; tests concept retrieval and recommendation under health-literacy variation. | Extends from one treatment-selection task to diagnosis/triage/safety and makes matched counterfactual case families the primary design, not independent simulated patients. | Show same hidden case across profile variants; report decision-invariance, safety-escalation, and calibration failures that patient-sim1-style aggregate profile scoring would not isolate. |
| PatientSim | MIMIC-IV/ED/Note-based ED patient profiles with 37 persona combinations; evaluates doctor LLMs for factual accuracy and persona consistency. | PatientSim asks whether personas are realistic and factual. This work asks whether clinical AI decisions change inappropriately when only profile presentation changes. | Reproduce a PatientSim-like persona baseline, then add matched counterfactual variants and show additional failure modes: changed diagnosis, urgency, confidence, or evidence use for the same case. |
| AgentClinic | Multi-agent clinical environment with patient, doctor, measurement, moderator, tools, specialties, languages, and bias perturbations. | AgentClinic evaluates sequential clinical competence and tool use. ### Reviewer Objections and Required Answers

| Reviewer objection | Why it is a real risk | Required answer in the study |
|---|---|---|
| "This is just recombining existing simulators and benchmarks." | The field already has patient simulators, agent benchmarks, and safety rubrics. | Make the primary object a **matched case family**, not a simulated encounter. Report metrics unavailable in ordinary case scoring: Counterfactual Profile Stability Index, Appropriate Adaptation Score, Questioning Equity Score, Explanation Leakage Score. |
| "Counterfactual is just prompt perturbation." | Literacy, recall, affect, and language can change what information is available, not only wording. | Define interventions formally: invariant clinical facts, mutable expression fields, allowed disclosure changes, and expected invariant outputs. Clinicians must confirm variants preserve the same diagnosis and urgency where labeled invariant. |
| "Your stress axes are a grab bag." | Too many axes without theory will look arbitrary. | Use a taxonomy: communication access (literacy, language), information reliability (recall, confusion), interaction behavior (distracted, minimizing, adversarial), social/demographic labels (race, sex/gender, insurance), and clinical modifiers (pregnancy, allergy, renal function). Label each axis as clinically irrelevant, clinically relevant, or communication/access relevant per case. |
| "DDXPlus + Synthea is synthetic-on-synthetic." | Failures may reflect synthetic artifacts rather than real clinical robustness. | Position DDXPlus/Synthea as the reproducible MVP only. Add a required validation extension using MIMIC-IV-ED/Note or at least PatientSim/MTS-Dialog/MedDialog/ACI-Bench-style dialogue validation. |
| "Holding clinical state fixed is unrealistic." | Real low recall or language barriers affect disclosure timing and available evidence. | Do not require identical utterances. Require identical hidden state and controlled disclosure policy. Measure whether the agent asks enough follow-up questions to recover key evidence under degraded communication. |
| "You confuse prompt sensitivity with clinical bias." | A model might react to wording, not profile unfairness. | Include ablations: neutral paraphrase, profile-preserving rewrite, explicit-profile vs hidden-profile, single-axis vs multi-axis, single-turn vs multi-turn. Attribute failures only when they survive these controls. |
| "Demographic variation is superficial." | Demographics are not just labels; they interact with prevalence, access, and social context. | Make demographic-only claims narrow. For clinically irrelevant demographic variants, hold anatomy/exposure/prevalence-relevant facts fixed. For clinically relevant demographic variants, pre-label expected changes and score appropriate adaptation, not invariance. |
| "Existing benchmarks could simply add perturbations." | This can look like a feature, not a new benchmark paradigm. | Show that adding case-family counterfactuals changes evaluation conclusions: model rankings, safety error rates, or discovered failure modes differ from ordinary accuracy/rubric scores. |
| "Simulated failures do not prove real clinical harm." | Simulation is not deployment evidence. | Claim only risk identification. Add clinician harm-potential ratings for failures, and report simulated stress-test findings as pre-deployment risk signals. |

### Formal Study Roadmap That Defends the Gap

**Unit of analysis:** a case family.

Each case family contains:

- `case_id`
- invariant clinical facts: diagnosis/pathology, symptoms, antecedents, medications, comorbidities, severity, red flags
- expected outputs: final diagnosis, acceptable differential, urgency/escalation, key next questions, contraindications, safety-net advice
- mutable patient-profile fields: literacy, recall, affect, language, behavior, demographic labels, access context
- per-axis label: clinically irrelevant, clinically relevant, or communication/access relevant
- generated encounters across all counterfactual profiles

**Core intervention:** change one patient-profile axis at a time while preserving the hidden clinical state. Then test intersectional variants after single-axis effects are understood.

**Target agent task:** up to 8-turn diagnostic/triage intake. The agent must ask questions, extract evidence, produce top-5 differential, final diagnosis, urgency level, recommended next action, confidence, missing-information list, and safety-net advice.

**Required metrics:**

| Metric | Definition | Why it proves novelty |
|---|---|---|
| Counterfactual Profile Stability Index (CPSI) | Stability of diagnosis, urgency, recommendation, and confidence across clinically irrelevant profile variants. | Directly measures the proposed gap: profile-conditioned instability. |
| Appropriate Adaptation Score (AAS) | Correct output change when the profile variable is clinically relevant, e.g., pregnancy, allergy, anticoagulant use. | Prevents the false claim that all profile sensitivity is bias. |
| Safety Error Rate (SER) | Missed escalation, unsafe reassurance, unsafe medication advice, missing red-flag follow-up. | Moves beyond realism into clinical risk. |
| Questioning Equity Score (QES) | Whether the agent asks equally useful follow-up questions across equivalent profiles. | Captures failures before final diagnosis. |
| Evidence Extraction F1 | Whether the agent captures symptoms, meds, antecedents, and red flags from patient language. | Connects patient expression to downstream errors. |
| Explanation Leakage Score (ELS) | Whether irrelevant demographics or stereotypes appear in the rationale. | Identifies unsupported demographic reasoning even when output is correct. |
| Calibration Shift | Confidence difference across variants with same hidden state. | Shows profile-conditioned over/under-confidence. |

**Minimum defensible experiment:**

1. Build 300 DDXPlus base cases, enriched with Synthea-style EHR context.
2. Generate 6 variants per case: baseline/proficient, low literacy, low recall, anxious/repetitive, distracted, adversarial/minimizing.
3. Run 3-5 clinical AI agents through the same multi-turn task.
4. Compare ordinary diagnosis accuracy with case-family CPSI, SER, QES, and calibration shift.
5. Have clinicians review at least 100 case families for invariant clinical state, expected escalation, and harm potential.

**Strong full experiment:**

1. Repeat the MVP on MIMIC-IV-ED/Note-derived cases if credentialed access is available.
2. Add PatientSim-like ED persona baselines to show what is gained by explicit counterfactual case families.
3. Validate simulator utterances against MTS-Dialog, MedDialog, or ACI-Bench distributions for length, uncertainty markers, medical term density, disclosure timing, and clinician plausibility.
4. Report whether counterfactual stress testing changes model rankings or reveals safety failures not captured by ordinary accuracy, HealthBench-style rubric scoring, or single-profile simulation.

### Claims the Paper Can Safely Make If the Roadmap Succeeds

- Existing patient simulation and clinical-agent benchmarks do not fully isolate profile-conditioned decision instability because they usually score independent cases or broad scenario performance.
- Matched counterfactual case families can identify whether an AI agent changes diagnosis, escalation, questioning, confidence, or explanation when hidden clinical facts are held constant.
- Some profile sensitivity is clinically appropriate; therefore, the benchmark separates invariance-required axes from adaptation-required axes.
- DDXPlus/Synthea provide a reproducible MVP substrate, while MIMIC-IV-ED/Note or real-dialogue validation is needed for stronger ecological validity.

### Claims to Avoid

- Do not claim to be the first patient simulator.
- Do not claim to prove deployed clinical safety.
- Do not claim all demographic sensitivity is bias.
- Do not claim DDXPlus/Synthea alone validates real-world clinical behavior.
- Do not claim clinician review can be replaced by LLM judges.

## 9. Minimum Viable Study Design

**Dataset:** DDXPlus + a small Synthea EHR context for each synthetic patient.

**Sample:** 300 base patients across 20-40 conditions. Generate 5 counterfactual profiles per patient: proficient, low literacy, vague recall, anxious/repetitive, adversarial/minimizing. Total 1,500 encounters.

**Simulator:** LLM patient constrained by structured hidden state. Every utterance should include internal trace tags that are stripped before the target AI sees the message.

**Target AI:** One or more open or API LLM agents that conduct up to 8 turns of diagnostic intake and output differential, final diagnosis, escalation decision, and confidence.

**Metrics:** Diagnosis accuracy, DDx recall, ask-next-question quality, escalation recall, unsafe reassurance rate, counterfactual decision invariance, confidence calibration, missing-information handling, and subgroup gaps by profile.

**Validation:** 2 clinicians review 100 encounter traces and 100 final recommendations. Use LLM judge only after comparing against clinician labels.

## 10. Stronger Full Study Design

**Phase 1:** Public MVP with DDXPlus/Synthea and 1,500-3,000 encounters.

**Phase 2:** Real EHR extension with MIMIC-IV-ED/Note: construct ED profiles from chief complaint, triage, diagnoses, meds, discharge-summary HPI/PMH, and outcomes. Use profile dimensions similar to PatientSim but add patient-sim1-style health literacy and behavioral controls.

**Phase 3:** Real-dialogue validation: compare simulator utterances to MedDialog, MTS-Dialog, and ACI-Bench on response length, medical term density, disclosure timing, uncertainty markers, affect, and clinician plausibility.

**Phase 4:** Safety/fairness audit: matched counterfactual profiles where hidden clinical facts are identical. Report whether AI changes escalation, diagnosis, confidence, or treatment advice solely because patient language or behavior changed.

**Full-study contribution:** A reusable benchmark and evaluation protocol for patient-simulation-based risk assessment of healthcare AI that is more reproducible than All of Us-only work and broader than antidepressant selection.

## 11. Safe Claims and Claims to Avoid

Safe claims:

- patient-sim1 demonstrates that controlled simulated patients can expose health-literacy-related degradation in a conversational healthcare AI decision aid.
- The clearest open gap is external validation of simulators against real patient conversations and broader AI tasks.
- DDXPlus, Synthea, MIMIC-IV, HealthBench, AgentClinic, PatientSim, and MedAgentBench have currently clear official access or project paths.
- NHATS should not be used as a primary recommendation until access is re-verified because the official site currently says data files are temporarily unavailable.
- Counterfactual profile testing is a practical way to measure robustness and equity because hidden clinical facts can be held constant.

Claims to avoid:

- Do not claim simulated patients are equivalent to real patients without validation.
- Do not claim All of Us profiles can be reproduced by outside researchers without Workbench access.
- Do not claim NHATS is available right now.
- Do not claim safety or fairness improvement unless the target AI is modified and re-tested.
- Do not claim LLM judges replace clinician review; use them as scalable screeners after agreement checks.
- Do not cite SDBench/NEJM CPC as a fully open dataset unless public access/licensing is confirmed.
